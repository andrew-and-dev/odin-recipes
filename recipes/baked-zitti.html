Baked Ziti

Home page.

Description

This easy baked ziti with ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan is so good! Three cheeses layered with pasta and marinara sauce — delicious!

Designed by Jody Richards.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ziti pasta
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for pasta water)
  • 1 pound Italian sausage (mild or spicy)
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 28 oz crushed tomatoes
  • 15 oz tomato sauce
  • 6 oz tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella (divided)
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil for the pasta.
  2. Cook the ziti according to package directions until al dente. Drain and toss with a drizzle of olive oil to prevent sticking. Set aside.
  3. While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the Italian sausage and ground beef, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Cook until browned, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Add the diced onion to the meat and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, basil, oregano, and sugar to the skillet. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  6. In a medium bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, chopped parsley, 1 cup of mozzarella, and 1/2 cup of Parmesan. Mix well.
  7. In a large bowl, combine the cooked ziti with the meat sauce. Fold in the ricotta mixture until everything is well combined.
  8. Transfer the pasta mixture to a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Spread evenly and top with the remaining 1 cup of mozzarella and 1/4 cup of Parmesan.
  9. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  10. Let the baked ziti rest for 10 minutes before serving. This allows the cheese to set and makes it easier to portion.
