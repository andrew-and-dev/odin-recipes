Reuben Sandwich

Home page.

Description

The classic Reuben sandwich with tender corned beef, tangy sauerkraut, melted Swiss cheese, and creamy Russian dressing all grilled between slices of buttery rye bread. This deli favorite is crispy on the outside and loaded with flavor on the inside!

Designed by Daniel Torobekov

Ingredients

  • 8 slices rye bread
  • 1 pound corned beef, thinly sliced
  • 8 slices Swiss cheese
  • 1 cup sauerkraut, drained and squeezed dry
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons sweet pickle relish
  • 1 teaspoon horseradish
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Make the Russian dressing by combining mayonnaise, ketchup, pickle relish, horseradish, and paprika in a small bowl. Mix well and season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Drain the sauerkraut thoroughly and squeeze out as much liquid as possible using paper towels or a clean kitchen towel.
  3. Spread butter on one side of each slice of rye bread.
  4. On the unbuttered side of 4 bread slices, spread a generous amount of Russian dressing.
  5. Layer each dressed slice with 2 slices of Swiss cheese, a portion of corned beef (about 4 ounces per sandwich), and 1/4 cup of drained sauerkraut.
  6. Top with another slicwe of Swiss cheese and close the sandwich with the remaining bread slices, buttered side facing out.
  7. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat.
  8. Place the sandwiches in the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, pressing down gently with a spatula, until the bread is golden brown and crispy and the cheese is melted.
  9. Remove from heat and let rest for 1 minute before cutting diagonally.
  10. Serve hot with pickle spears and potato chips on the side.
